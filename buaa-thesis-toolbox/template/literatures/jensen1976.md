## Abstract
Develops a theory of the ownership structure of the firm. Defines agency costs arising from the separation of ownership and control, and analyzes how outside equity and debt contracts allocate residual claims and monitoring rights. Provides the canonical framing for subsequent corporate-governance research on ownership concentration, managerial incentives, and bonding mechanisms.

## Reading Notes
> Agency costs equal the sum of monitoring expenditures by the principal, bonding expenditures by the agent, and the residual loss.

**Note:** Useful one-sentence definition for Ch.2 / Ch.3 theoretical background.

> The firm is a nexus for a set of contracting relationships among individuals.

**Note:** Emphasize “contracting nexus” when contrasting structure vs. process in the StarVoyage case.

> As the owner-manager’s fraction of equity falls, the incentive to appropriate perquisites rises.

**Note:** Maps to the thesis’s Top1 / ownership-dispersion discussion in Ch.5.
