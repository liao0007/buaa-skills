## Abstract
Examines the association between board independence and earnings quality in emerging-market listed firms. Finds that higher independent-director ratios are associated with lower discretionary accruals when audit committees are active, suggesting that independence improves reporting quality only conditionally on board-process effectiveness.

## Reading Notes
> Independence is necessary but not sufficient; committee diligence mediates the independence–earnings quality link.

**Note:** Aligns with the thesis claim that “headcount independence” without substantive challenge is weak.

> Effects are stronger after regulatory reforms that clarify independent directors’ duties.
